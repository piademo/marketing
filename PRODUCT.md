# Bookfast — Product Context

## Register
brand

## Product Purpose
Bookfast is appointment management software for barbershops, hair salons, and aesthetics centers in Spain. The product eliminates manual scheduling via WhatsApp, reduces no-shows with automatic reminders, and gives independent beauty professionals full control of their agenda without complexity.

## Users
Independent barbershops and salons (1–15 staff). Owners are hands-on — they cut hair, do treatments, and manage the business simultaneously. They are practical, time-poor, and skeptical of software complexity. They don't want tech — they want their schedule to fill itself. Age 28–55. Spanish-speaking. Often mobile-first.

## Brand Tone
Direct, confident, human. Talks to barbers and stylists like a peer who's solved the same problem. Never corporate. Never feature-list-heavy. Benefits before features. Urgency around lost revenue (no-shows, missed calls) not hype around technology.

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS landing pages with hero-metric grids (big numbers, small labels)
- Over-animated pages that feel like tech demos
- Clinical or cold aesthetics (healthcare white, fintech navy)
- Pages that look like they were designed with a Figma SaaS template

## Color Strategy
Committed — sky blue is the primary identity color carrying 30–60% of key surfaces. Purple is a complementary accent for depth and gradient endpoints. The palette should feel energetic but trustworthy, not loud.

## Brand Colors
- Primary: oklch(63% 0.18 220) — sky blue #0ea5e9
- Accent: oklch(65% 0.22 296) — purple #a855f7
- Background light: oklch(99% 0.003 240) — warm white
- Background dark: oklch(10% 0.005 240) — near black with blue tint
- Text dark: oklch(18% 0.02 255) — deep blue-slate

## Typography
- Font: Inter (variable)
- Hero headings: weight 800–900, tracking -0.03em
- Section headings: weight 700, tracking -0.02em
- Body: weight 400–500, 1.6 line-height
- No gradient text — use solid primary or accent color for emphasis

## Strategic Principles
1. Every section must answer "what's in it for me" before "what does it do"
2. Social proof is more convincing than features — prioritize numbers and outcomes
3. The booking demo (ProDashboardDemo) is the hero — let it do the selling
4. Friction must be zero: every CTA leads directly to a signup or demo
5. Mobile converts — the mobile experience must be as good as desktop
